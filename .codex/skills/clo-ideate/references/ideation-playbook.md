# Ideation Playbook

This playbook defines the recommended early-stage ideation loop for Dittonomics.

It is inspired by two upstream patterns:

- structured idea-evaluation pipelines that force explicit question, threat, and next-step thinking
- research-companion style ideation sessions that move from divergence to decision instead of stopping at brainstorming

The Dittonomics adaptation keeps only the pieces that fit the Clo architecture.

## Phase 1: Seed

Clarify the starting point.

Minimum questions:

- what phenomenon, puzzle, policy, or dataset is motivating this idea?
- why does this matter now?
- what does the user already know, suspect, or have access to?
- what constraint is most binding: data, methods, setting, time, or novelty?

Output:

- `idea_brief`

## Phase 2: Diverge

Generate candidate directions instead of committing too early.

Try multiple axes:

- question-first
- dataset-first
- mechanism-first
- institutional-angle-first
- surprising-comparison or cross-field transfer

Target:

- 3 to 7 candidate research questions

## Phase 3: Evaluate

Pressure-test the candidate questions using the ideation rubric.

At this stage, do not do a full literature review.
Do enough closest-paper checking to avoid obvious duplication and to identify the real threat surface.

Output:

- ranked shortlist
- strongest objection
- best pivot if the first version fails

## Phase 4: Deepen

Take the best 1 to 2 questions and add empirical traction.

For each serious candidate, write:

- one-sentence research question
- hypothesis
- likely treatment or variation
- identification sketch
- data sketch
- why-you or why-now advantage
- one fast-fail test

## Phase 5: Frame

Write the idea in a way that could survive contact with a skeptical reader.

Minimum framing outputs:

- one-sentence nugget
- why-now line
- closest-paper threat line
- contribution hook

## Phase 6: Decide

Make a clear decision:

- `pursue`
- `refine`
- `park`
- `kill`

Every decision should include:

- rationale
- next discovery step if pursued
- key risk to monitor
- fallback pivot if relevant

## Boundary With Discovery

Ideation is not discovery.

Ideation should stop short of:

- exhaustive literature mapping
- ranked bibliography collection
- full data-source search
- full research-spec formalization when the question is still unstable

Once the question survives screening, move to `clo-discover`.
